// How EcoViewer is organised

Five thematic
analysis layers

EcoViewer is organised around five thematic panels, each grouping related environmental variables into a coherent view. Select a location on the map and switch between panels to explore different dimensions of the ecosystem at that point.

All layers are derived from peer-reviewed, publicly available datasets processed through Google Earth Engine. Citations for every dataset are listed in the section below.

01

Climatic Info

Long-term climate variables including mean annual rainfall, temperature seasonality, and aridity indices. Useful for characterising the broad climatic envelope of an ecosystem.

RainfallTemperatureAridity
02

Vegetation

Structural and compositional vegetation attributes including land cover classification, tree canopy cover, and grass layer characteristics. Core inputs for ecological characterisation.

Land CoverTree CanopyGrass Layer
03

Waterbodies & Wetlands

Surface water and wetland extent, including wetland classification, mapped rivers and lakes, and seasonal inundation patterns. Essential context for riparian and aquatic ecosystems.

Wetland ClassRiversLakes
04

Fire Disturbance

Historical and recent fire occurrence data, including burn frequency and fire radiative power. Important for understanding disturbance regimes and ecosystem dynamics over time.

Burn FrequencyFire Radiative Power
05

GBIF Occurrences

Species occurrence records from the Global Biodiversity Information Facility (GBIF), cross-referenced to ecosystem types using peer-reviewed ecological literature. Helps contextualise biodiversity within the broader landscape.

Species OccurrencesEcosystem Cross-reference
